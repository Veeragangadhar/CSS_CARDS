SUJATHA DEVELOPERS

Low Budget Constructions

Casual houses

The mid-housing segment is gaining popularity, thanks to developers providing projects, societies, community clubs, and function spaces. According to a recent report by Anarock, 59% of Indians prefer homes priced between Rs 45 lakh and Rs 1.5 crore, despite the rise in residential prices.


Appartment

Flats

One-bedroom apartments consist of a separate bedroom, living room, kitchen, and bathroom. They offer more privacy and space compared to studio apartments, making them suitable for couples and small families. These apartments strike a balance between affordability and comfort. They often come with basic amenities such as a balcony or a small garden space, providing a more comfortable living experience. One-bedroom apartments are a popular choice for those looking to move up from a studio while still managing rental costs. However, they do come with higher rent and utility costs compared to studios, and the space might still be limited for larger families.


Residential Houses

Single-Family Homes

Single-Family Homes: These are standalone structures designed to house one family. They often come with a yard and are popular in suburban areas. They can vary in architectural styles, including ranch, colonial, and contemporary designs.


Gated Communities Constructions

Luxury houses

Apart from security personnel guarding the entrance round the clock, gated communities boast amenities such as club houses, swimming pools, community halls, play grounds, sports club, etc. One will have power back-up and parking facilities if they are a member.


Corporate Constructions

Business

Corporate architecture refers to the use of architectural design to construct physical spaces (buildings, workplaces, etc.) that can promote the image of a corporation.[1] During the 20th century corporate architecture underwent a transformation from functional design philosophies to more creative endeavours, which are able to be an expression of the firm’s institutional identity and play a role in stakeholders’ image of the organisation
